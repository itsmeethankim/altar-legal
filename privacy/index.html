Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 29, 2026

Altar ("we", "our", "the app") is a Christian discipline app that intercepts distracting app use with Scripture, reflection, and prayer. This policy explains what information Altar collects, why, and your choices.

The short version

  • Altar is local-first. Your data stays on your device.
  • We do not sell your data. We do not track you across apps or the web. We do not run ads.
  • We do not ask for your name, email, phone number, or any contact information.
  • Your Screen Time app selections never leave your device.
  • The Bible Assistant feature uses an anonymous, device-bound session to talk to our backend — it has no link to your real-world identity.

What we collect

On-device only (never leaves your phone)

  • Your selected blocked apps (Screen Time / Family Controls data).
  • Your reflections, prayer notes, and intervention history.
  • Cached Bible text and reading plan content bundled with the app.
  • Reading-plan progress, settings, streaks, and unlock history.
  • OCR'd passage text from Scan to Unlock.

Anonymous session identifier

On first launch, the app creates an anonymous session with our backend (Supabase). This session is a random identifier stored only on your device. It is not linked to your name, email, Apple ID, phone, or any other identifying information. We use it solely to:

  • Authenticate your requests to the Bible Assistant feature.
  • Apply per-user rate limits so the feature remains affordable for everyone.

If you reinstall the app, a brand-new anonymous identifier is generated. The old one is unrecoverable and unattributable.

Purchase records

If you buy a subscription, Apple processes the transaction. Altar receives only an anonymous entitlement status (premium on/off) from Apple's StoreKit — we never see your payment details.

Bible Assistant (optional AI feature)

If you use the in-app Bible Assistant, your prompt and the relevant verse context are sent to our Supabase Edge Function over HTTPS. The Edge Function records the timestamp of the request (so we can rate-limit abuse); the prompt text itself is not stored server-side. The prompt and passage are forwarded to OpenAI's API to generate a response. OpenAI does not see any identifying information about you. Under OpenAI's standard API data-usage policy, prompts sent via the API are not used to train OpenAI's models. If the Edge Function is unavailable, Altar falls back to offline responses that never leave your device.

What we do NOT collect

  • Your real name, email address, phone number, or any contact information.
  • Your Apple ID or sign-in tokens.
  • Which specific apps you have blocked.
  • Photos, camera content, or location.
  • Any cross-device sync or cloud backup of your spiritual data.

How we use your information

  • To run the app's core features (interventions, streaks, reading plans).
  • To rate-limit the AI Bible Assistant fairly across users.
  • To improve the app's reliability by aggregating anonymous crash and error data (iOS system-level only — we do not run third-party analytics SDKs).

How we protect your information

  • All network traffic uses HTTPS/TLS.
  • The anonymous session identifier carries no link to your identity.
  • Per-IP and per-anonymous-user rate limits prevent abuse of the AI backend.
  • On-device data is stored via Apple's SwiftData framework, encrypted by iOS at rest.

Data transfer when you upgrade your phone

When you transfer to a new iPhone via Quick Start, iCloud Backup, or an encrypted local backup, Altar's on-device data (history, reflections, settings, reading-plan progress) is included in the standard iOS migration and carries over automatically. If you set up your new phone "as new" without restoring a backup, that data does not transfer because Altar does not sync to the cloud.

Your choices

  • Reset all app data in Settings → Account → About → Reset All App Data. This erases every reflection, scan, and setting on this device.
  • Revoke Screen Time access in iOS Settings → Screen Time → Family Controls. Altar will continue to work with a limited feature set.
  • Revoke camera access in iOS Settings → Privacy → Camera. Scan to Unlock will be unavailable.
  • Delete the app to remove all on-device data. Because we never collect cloud copies of your spiritual data, no further deletion request is needed — there is nothing else to delete.

Children

Altar is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from them.

Third parties

  • Supabase (backend): hosts our Edge Function for the Bible Assistant. Receives only the anonymous session identifier and request timestamps. See supabase.com/privacy.
  • OpenAI (optional, via our Edge Function): processes Bible Assistant prompts if that feature is used. Receives only the prompt content, not your identity. See openai.com/policies/privacy-policy.
  • Apple (StoreKit): processes purchases.

Changes

We will update this policy as the app evolves. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent version.
